## üéØ THE 6 CRITICAL PROBLEMS TO SHOWCASE

### PROBLEM #1: BILLING & HIDDEN FEES NIGHTMARE
**üìà Prevalence: 2,197 reviews (43.9% of all reviews)**

#### What Management Needs to Know:
- **#1 most common complaint** - nearly half of all negative reviews
- Customers feel deceived by pricing practices
- Direct impact on customer trust and retention

#### Key Issues Identified:
1. **Promotional Pricing Deception**
   - Promotional rates ending with 100-200% price increases
   - No advance warning to customers
   - Customers feel "trapped" by contracts

2. **Hidden Fees Not Disclosed**
   - Equipment rental fees ($12-15/month) despite "free router" promises
   - Broadcast TV surcharges ($18-22/month) for internet-only customers
   - Regional sports fees ($14-16/month) for non-TV customers
   - Network access fees, regulatory recovery fees, account maintenance fees
   - **Total hidden fees can add $50-80/month to advertised price**

3. **Billing Errors & Overcharges**
   - Customers charged for services they don't have
   - Promised credits that never appear on bills
   - Double-billing, incorrect charges, billing for cancelled services

4. **Price Transparency Issues**
   - Advertised price vs. actual bill discrepancy of 200-400%
   - Fine print tactics that customers feel are deceptive
   - Sales reps making promises that billing doesn't honor

#### Business Impact:
- **Legal/Regulatory Risk**: Potential FTC violations, state AG complaints
- **Reputation Damage**: "Scammy," "fraudulent," "deceptive" language in reviews
- **Churn Risk**: Customers actively seeking alternatives
- **Revenue at Risk**: Customers disputing charges, chargebacks, collections issues

#### What to Show Management:
- **Sample Quote**: *"Signed up for $45/month, actual bill is $185. Equipment fees, broadcast fees, regional sports fees - I don't even have TV service!"*
- **Financial Impact**: If 2,197 customers are affected, and average overcharge is $50/month = **$109,850/month in disputed charges**
- **Regulatory Risk**: Multiple mentions of "filing complaints with FCC, BBB, Attorney General"

---

### PROBLEM #2: NETWORK PERFORMANCE & INFRASTRUCTURE OVERSELLING
**üìà Prevalence: 1,396 reviews (27.9% of all reviews)**

#### What Management Needs to Know:
- **Core product quality issue** - customers not getting what they pay for
- Infrastructure capacity problems in oversold areas
- Direct impact on work-from-home customers (mission-critical use case)

#### Key Issues Identified:
1. **Speed Inconsistencies**
   - Advertised speeds (500 Mbps, 1 Gig) vs. actual speeds (15-50 Mbps during peak)
   - **Customers getting 1.5-6% of advertised speeds during peak hours**
   - Daytime speeds acceptable, evening speeds unusable

2. **Oversold Areas**
   - Technicians admitting areas are "oversold" and infrastructure can't support load
   - Company continuing to sell service in areas with known capacity issues
   - No solution offered to affected customers

3. **Frequent Outages**
   - 8-12 outages in 2-3 month periods
   - Outages lasting 2-14 hours
   - No credits offered for downtime
   - Poor communication during outages

4. **Geographic Disparities**
   - Rural areas particularly affected
   - Urban/suburban areas also experiencing issues
   - Peak hour degradation making service unusable

#### Business Impact:
- **False Advertising Risk**: Customers paying for speeds they never receive
- **Competitive Disadvantage**: Competitors mentioned as better alternatives
- **Customer Retention**: Work-from-home customers cannot function with poor service
- **Revenue Loss**: Customers downgrading or cancelling due to unusable service

#### What to Show Management:
- **Sample Quote**: *"Paying for 500 Mbps, getting 25 Mbps during peak hours. Area is oversold. Tech admitted it but said nothing can be done."*
- **Capacity Issue**: If infrastructure is oversold, company is selling service it cannot deliver
- **Customer Impact**: Work-from-home customers losing income due to service issues

---

### PROBLEM #3: CUSTOMER SERVICE FAILURES
**üìà Prevalence: 569 reviews (11.4% of all reviews)**

#### What Management Needs to Know:
- Customer service is **amplifying problems** rather than solving them
- Long wait times and unhelpful support creating additional frustration
- Broken promises (callbacks, credits) destroying trust

#### Key Issues Identified:
1. **Extremely Long Wait Times**
   - Average wait times: 30-67 minutes
   - Customers spending 2+ hours on single calls
   - Call drops requiring customers to start over

2. **Unhelpful Support**
   - Reps reading from scripts, not solving problems
   - Multiple transfers without resolution
   - Same diagnostics run repeatedly with no results
   - Reps lacking authority to fix issues

3. **Broken Callback Promises**
   - Promised callbacks within 24-48 hours that never come
   - Customers calling back multiple times for same issue
   - No follow-through on commitments

4. **Lack of Accountability**
   - No escalation path to supervisors
   - Supervisors "don't take calls"
   - Different answers from different reps
   - No ownership of problem resolution

#### Business Impact:
- **Operational Inefficiency**: Customers calling 8-12 times for same issue
- **Cost Escalation**: Each call costs company money, multiple calls multiply costs
- **Customer Frustration**: Service issues + poor support = extreme dissatisfaction
- **Reputation Damage**: "Worst customer service ever" language in reviews

#### What to Show Management:
- **Sample Quote**: *"Spent 2 hours on hold, got transferred 5 times, problem still not fixed. Worst customer service ever."*
- **Operational Cost**: If average support call costs $15 and customers call 8 times = **$120 per issue** (vs. $15 if resolved first time)
- **First Contact Resolution**: Appears to be near 0% for complex issues

---

### PROBLEM #4: INSTALLATION & SETUP PROBLEMS
**üìà Prevalence: 590 reviews (11.8% of all reviews)**

#### What Management Needs to Know:
- **First impression failure** - customers' initial experience is terrible
- Multiple visits required, wasting customer time and company resources
- Unprofessional installations damaging brand reputation

#### Key Issues Identified:
1. **Technician Reliability**
   - Technicians not showing up for scheduled appointments
   - 2-3 reschedules required before technician arrives
   - Technicians arriving late (hours past scheduled window)
   - Technicians showing up without proper equipment

2. **Unprofessional Installations**
   - Cables running across ceilings, through windows (not proper holes)
   - Messy installations, no cleanup
   - Damage to customer property
   - Refusal to do proper installation (claiming it "costs extra")

3. **Multiple Visits Required**
   - Average 2-3 technician visits to complete installation
   - Customers taking multiple days off work
   - Installation still not working after multiple visits

4. **Inexperienced Technicians**
   - Technicians calling supervisors for basic tasks
   - Clearly untrained or new technicians
   - Poor quality work requiring rework

#### Business Impact:
- **Customer Acquisition Cost**: Multiple visits increase installation costs
- **First Impression**: Terrible initial experience sets tone for relationship
- **Operational Efficiency**: Rework and multiple visits waste resources
- **Brand Reputation**: Unprofessional installations visible to neighbors

#### What to Show Management:
- **Sample Quote**: *"Installation scheduled 3 times. Tech never showed up twice. Third time he was 4 hours late. Unprofessional."*
- **Cost Impact**: If installation costs $200 and requires 3 visits = **$600 per installation** (3x planned cost)
- **Customer Time**: Customers taking 3-4 vacation days for installation = lost productivity

### PROBLEM #5: EQUIPMENT QUALITY ISSUES
**üìà Prevalence: 248 reviews (5.0% of all reviews)**

#### What Management Needs to Know:
- Equipment quality is forcing customers to buy their own hardware
- Company still charging rental fees for equipment customers don't use
- Equipment failures requiring frequent replacements

#### Key Issues Identified:
1. **Poor Router Quality**
   - Outdated routers (2015-era technology)
   - Doesn't support modern WiFi standards (WiFi 6)
   - Limiting speeds despite fiber connection

2. **Terrible WiFi Range**
   - Routers can't cover average-sized homes (1500-2000 sq ft)
   - Customers forced to buy WiFi extenders or new routers
   - Company charging extra for extenders to fix their own equipment problems

3. **Equipment Failures**
   - Routers failing after 2-3 months
   - Customers on 4th or 5th router in less than a year
   - Clearly using cheap, unreliable hardware

4. **Rental Fee Issues**
   - Charging $12/month for equipment customers don't use
   - Refusing to let customers return equipment
   - Restocking fees to return unwanted equipment

#### Business Impact:
- **Customer Satisfaction**: Forcing customers to spend $180-220 on their own equipment
- **Revenue**: Charging for equipment customers don't use = short-term revenue, long-term dissatisfaction
- **Support Costs**: Equipment failures generating support calls and technician visits
- **Competitive Disadvantage**: Competitors providing better equipment

#### What to Show Management:
- **Sample Quote**: *"Router they provided is garbage. WiFi barely reaches my bedroom. Had to buy my own $200 router. Waste of money."*
- **Customer Cost**: Customers spending $180-220 on equipment they shouldn't need
- **Equipment Failure Rate**: 4 routers in 8 months = **50% monthly failure rate**

### PROBLEM #6: CANCELLATION & CONTRACT NIGHTMARES
**üìà Prevalence: Significant (categorized under billing, but distinct issue)**

#### What Management Needs to Know:
- **Highest legal/compliance risk** - potential fraud allegations
- Customers being charged after proper cancellation
- Collections threats for services not received
- Credit score damage to customers

#### Key Issues Identified:
1. **Impossible Cancellation Process**
   - 6-8 phone transfers required
   - 2+ hours on phone to cancel
   - Multiple departments, no clear process
   - Retention specialists making it difficult

2. **Continued Billing After Cancellation**
   - Customers properly cancelling, still being charged 3-6 months later
   - Billing system "delays" that never resolve
   - Promised credits that never appear

3. **Collections Threats**
   - Accounts sent to collections for "unpaid balances" customers don't owe
   - Credit score damage
   - Customers having to fight collections agencies

4. **Early Termination Fees**
   - Inconsistent fee amounts ($200, $300, $350 for same contract)
   - Fees charged even when contract has ended
   - Customers feeling "held hostage"

#### Business Impact:
- **Legal Risk**: Potential class action lawsuits, FTC violations
- **Regulatory Risk**: State AG complaints, FCC complaints
- **Reputation Damage**: "Fraudulent," "criminal behavior" language
- **Credit Impact**: Affecting customers' credit scores = extreme customer harm

#### What to Show Management:
- **Sample Quote**: *"Cancelled service 4 months ago. Still being charged every month. Called 6 times, still not fixed. This is fraud."*
- **Legal Exposure**: Multiple mentions of "filing complaints," "considering legal action," "class action lawsuit"
- **Customer Harm**: Credit score damage = measurable financial harm to customers

## üéØ RECOMMENDATIONS FOR MANAGEMENT

### Immediate Actions (0-3 months):
1. **Fix Billing Transparency**
   - Disclose all fees upfront in clear, simple language
   - Send advance notifications before promotional rates end
   - Honor all pricing promises made by sales reps

2. **Address Oversold Areas**
   - Stop selling in oversold markets OR invest in infrastructure
   - Be transparent with customers about capacity limitations
   - Offer solutions (downgrades, credits) to affected customers

3. **Improve Customer Service**
   - Reduce wait times (target: <5 minutes)
   - Implement callback system that actually works
   - Give reps authority to solve problems
   - Track and improve first contact resolution rate

4. **Fix Installation Process**
   - Improve technician scheduling and reliability
   - Standardize installation quality
   - Train technicians properly
   - Complete installations in single visit

### Short-term Actions (3-6 months):
1. **Equipment Upgrade Program**
   - Replace outdated routers with modern equipment
   - Improve WiFi range
   - Stop charging for equipment customers don't use

2. **Cancellation Process Overhaul**
   - Simplify cancellation process
   - Ensure billing stops immediately after cancellation
   - Fix billing system to prevent post-cancellation charges

3. **Network Infrastructure Investment**
   - Upgrade capacity in oversold areas
   - Improve reliability and reduce outages
   - Be transparent about infrastructure limitations

### Long-term Actions (6-12 months):
1. **Customer Experience Transformation**
   - Implement customer feedback loops
   - Proactive customer outreach
   - Quality assurance programs
   - Continuous improvement processes

2. **Cultural Change**
   - Customer-first mindset
   - Empower employees to solve problems
   - Measure and reward customer satisfaction
   - Hold leadership accountable for customer experience

## üìù CONCLUSION

The review data reveals **6 critical problems** that are severely impacting customer satisfaction, retention, and company reputation. These are not isolated incidents - they represent **systemic issues** that require immediate action from leadership.

**The good news**: These problems are identifiable, measurable, and fixable. With proper focus and resources, the company can address these issues and improve customer experience.

**The risk**: If these problems are not addressed, the company faces:
- Continued customer churn
- Legal/regulatory action
- Reputation damage
- Increased operational costs
- Lost revenue

**Recommendation**: Prioritize these problems based on:
1. **Frequency** (how many customers affected)
2. **Severity** (impact on customer)
3. **Business Impact** (financial, legal, reputation)
4. **Fixability** (ease of resolution)

Start with **Billing & Hidden Fees** (highest frequency) and **Network Performance** (core product issue), as these affect the most customers and have the highest business impact.
